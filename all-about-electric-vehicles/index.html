The spectrum of electric vehicles has expanded to encompass various modes of transportation, ranging from conventional cars to transit buses, diverse truck sizes, and substantial big-rig tractor-trailers, all propelled, at least in part, by electricity. Three main Types of Electric Vehicles can be broadly categorized: Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs): These vehicles gain power from electricity, which is stored in a battery pack. Plug-in Hybrids (PHEVs) combine a traditional gasoline or diesel engine with an electric motor and a sizable rechargeable battery, offering a dual power source. Fuel Cell Vehicles Operate by splitting electrons from hydrogen molecules to generate electricity for the motor, and they represent another innovative category. 2.
