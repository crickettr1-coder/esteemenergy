Best Solar Feed-in Tariff Brisbane: Maximize Your Solar Savings

If you're looking to reduce your electricity bills and make the most out of your solar energy system, finding the best solar feed-in tariff Brisbane is essential. A solar feed-in tariff (FIT) allows homeowners to sell excess solar electricity back to the grid, turning their rooftop solar into a financial asset. With Brisbane’s abundant sunlight, selecting the right energy provider with the best FIT can significantly increase your savings. Brisbane residents have access to a variety of feed-in tariff options from different electricity retailers. Some providers offer higher FIT rates but may impose daily caps, while others provide moderate FITs with flexible conditions. Additionally, government solar energy rebates and incentives further enhance the benefits of solar power. This comprehensive guide explores the top feed-in tariffs in Brisbane, explains how FITs work, and provides expert tips to maximize your solar earnings.

Understanding the Solar Feed-in Tariff

A solar feed-in tariff is a rate that energy providers pay for the excess solar power you generate and send back to the electricity grid. This allows homeowners to offset their electricity bills and potentially earn additional income from their solar systems.

How the Feed-in Tariff Works in Brisbane

  • Solar Panels Generate Power: During daylight hours, your rooftop solar system produces electricity.
  • Excess Energy is Exported: Any energy your home doesn’t immediately consume is sent to the grid.
  • Energy Credits Applied: Your electricity provider credits your account according to the Brisbane feed-in tariff rates.
  • Earnings Depend on Export & Tariff: The amount you earn depends on both the FIT rate and how much energy you export.
  • Battery Storage Option: Homeowners with solar battery systems can store excess energy instead of exporting it, reducing reliance on the grid and optimizing usage.
Brisbane’s feed-in tariff rates are determined by market competition, government policies, and energy retailer pricing strategies. While some providers offer up to 18 cents per kWh, others have more balanced plans with lower FITs but cheaper electricity rates. To get the most out of your solar system, consider installing a 6.6 kW solar system, which is one of the most popular options for residential solar users in Brisbane.

Top Energy Providers Offering the Best Solar Feed-in Tariff Brisbane

Several energy providers in Brisbane offer competitive feed-in tariffs. It is crucial to compare rates and conditions to ensure you choose the best plan for your needs. While some providers offer high FITs, they may have hidden costs such as higher electricity usage rates, limited daily export allowances, or restrictions on solar system sizes.

Mojo Power – Highest Feed-in Tariff Provider in Brisbane

Mojo Power offers the highest feed-in tariff in Brisbane, at 18.0 cents per kWh. However, this plan comes with a strict cap of 5 kWh per day, meaning if your solar system exports more than 5 kWh in a single day, the excess power is compensated at a lower rate. While Mojo Power’s FIT is attractive, homeowners should evaluate whether their solar generation and export habits align with this plan.

Discover Energy – A Strong Competitor in Solar FITs

Discover Energy provides a maximum feed-in tariff of 16.0 cents per kWh, making it a competitive option for Brisbane residents. The main drawback of this plan is that it is capped at 3.28 kWh per day, limiting the amount of power that can be sold at the highest rate. However, Discover Energy also offers a Virtual Power Plant (VPP) program, allowing customers with battery storage to benefit from additional savings.

AGL – Balanced Feed-in Tariff with Reliable Service

AGL is one of the largest electricity providers in Australia and offers a feed-in tariff of 12.0 cents per kWh under their Solar Savers Plan. This plan is only available to customers with a solar system up to 10 kW in size. While AGL’s FIT is not the highest, their overall electricity rates are competitive, making it a well-rounded option for homeowners looking for stable pricing without restrictive caps.

Red Energy – Competitive Mid-Range FITs

Red Energy provides a maximum FIT of 11.5 cents per kWh, but exports are capped at 5 kWh per day. This provider is backed by Snowy Hydro, making it an attractive choice for those looking for reliable service and sustainable energy solutions.

1st Energy – Good for Small to Medium Solar Systems

1st Energy offers a feed-in tariff of 11.0 cents per kWh, making it a viable option for homeowners with solar systems under 10 kW. However, customers should carefully review the provider’s usage rates and contract terms before signing up.

Other Providers with Decent Feed-in Tariffs

  • EnergyAustralia offers up to 10.0 cents per kWh, with special plans for larger solar systems.
  • Origin Energy provides a maximum FIT of 8.0 cents per kWh, with competitive electricity rates for customers who consume more energy during peak hours.
  • Alinta Energy has a feed-in tariff of 8.0 cents per kWh, but it is limited to homes with solar inverters under 5 kW.
Choosing the best solar feed-in tariff Brisbane depends on your solar export habits, system size, and daily energy consumption.

How to Maximize Your Solar Feed-in Tariff Earnings

To get the most financial benefit from your solar feed-in tariff, follow these expert tips:

Use More Solar Energy During the Day

Instead of exporting excess energy at a low FIT rate, try to consume as much solar power as possible while your system is actively generating electricity. Running appliances like washing machines, dishwashers, and air conditioners during daylight hours can help reduce reliance on grid electricity.

Install a Solar Battery

A solar panel battery storage system allows you to store excess solar energy instead of sending it to the grid at a low FIT rate. This stored power can then be used at night, further reducing electricity bills. Battery technology has improved significantly, and government rebates make solar battery storage more affordable than ever before.

Regularly Compare Feed-in Tariff Plans

Energy providers change their FIT rates frequently, so reviewing your plan every 6 to 12 months can help ensure you're getting the best deal. Some retailers introduce promotional offers with limited-time higher FIT rates, making it worthwhile to switch providers when needed.

Optimize Your Solar Panel Efficiency

The placement and angle of your solar panels affect energy generation. Ensuring panels are installed correctly, regularly cleaned, and free from shade will maximize solar production and increase export potential. For those looking to go completely off-grid, a 19.5 kW solar system is a great option.

Solar Energy Rebates and Incentives for Brisbane Homeowners

The Australian government provides solar energy rebates and financial incentives to encourage solar adoption. These rebates help lower installation costs, making solar power more affordable.

Available Rebates for Brisbane Homeowners

  • Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs): These government-backed certificates reduce the upfront cost of installing a solar PV system. The number of STCs depends on your solar system size and expected energy output.
  • Battery Rebates: Some households can access subsidies for solar battery storage systems, reducing the cost of adding a battery to their setup.
  • State-Based Solar Programs: Queensland offers additional solar energy incentives that can help further reduce installation and energy costs.
Taking advantage of these rebates can help homeowners maximize their return on investment while benefiting from the best solar feed-in tariff Brisbane.
