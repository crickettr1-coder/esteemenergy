One of the most significant components of govt subsidies for 10kW system Australia is the STC program. Small-scale Technology Certificates are issued based on your system’s size and expected energy output over 10 years.

  • Each STC is worth approximately $35–$40 in 2025

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. How many panels are needed for a 10kW solar system?

A 10kW system typically requires 24 to 28 solar panels, depending on the wattage of each panel (usually 370W to 420W per panel). Panel count may vary based on available roof space and layout.

Q2. What is the average size per panel?

Most residential solar panels are about 1.7m x 1m in size and deliver around 400W. For a 10kW system, you’d need approximately 40–50m² of roof space.

Q3. Does orientation affect the panel count?

Not the number, but it affects the efficiency of energy production. North-facing panels perform best in Australia. East/west orientation may require more panels or battery backup to reach similar output.

Q4. How do I qualify for govt subsidies for 10kW system Australia?

 Eligibility typically depends on:

  • Property ownership
  • System installed by a Clean Energy Council accredited provider
  • System size under 100kW

Esteem Energy checks all criteria for you during the consultation.

Q5. Are STC incentives available for every Australian state?

 Yes, STCs apply nationwide under the federal SRES scheme. However, the number of STCs may vary based on your postcode zone.

Q6. Can I still claim solar rebates Australia if I already have a smaller system?

 If you’re upgrading or replacing an older system, you may still qualify for new rebates. It depends on system capacity and installation date.

Q7. Are federal solar programs guaranteed long term?

 The STC scheme is scheduled to phase out by 2030, reducing value each year. That’s why acting now ensures you maximize your govt subsidies for 10kW system Australia.

Q8. What happens if I generate more power than I use?

 Your excess electricity is exported back to the grid, and you receive feed-in tariff credits, helping further reduce your electricity bill.
