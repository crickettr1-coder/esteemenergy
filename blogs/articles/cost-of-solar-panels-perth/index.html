How Much Do Solar Panels Cost in Perth?

The cost of solar panels in Perth depends on a range of factors, including system size, brand quality, inverter type, and installation complexity. While government rebates significantly reduce the initial investment, homeowners should still be aware of the actual solar panel price before making a purchase.

Solar Panel Pricing Breakdown in Perth (2025)

  • A 3 kW solar system costs between $3,000 and $4,500 after government rebates. It’s ideal for small households with low energy consumption.
  • A 5 kW solar system is priced between $4,500 and $7,000. It suits medium-sized households that use a moderate amount of electricity during the day.
  • A 6.6 kW solar system costs between $5,000 and $8,500 after rebates. This is the most common system size, balancing affordability and efficiency.
  • A 10.5 kW solar system ranges from $8,000 to $13,000 and is suitable for large homes or businesses with high electricity demands.

Factors Affecting the Cost of Solar Panels in Perth

  1. System Size – The larger the system, the more expensive it is upfront. However, bigger systems generate more electricity, leading to greater long-term savings.
  2. Solar Panel Efficiency and Brand – High-quality panels from brands like Trina, SunPower, Jinko, and LG offer better efficiency and durability, but they come at a premium price. Cheaper panels may degrade faster, requiring more frequent replacements.
  3. Inverter Type – Homeowners can choose between string inverters and microinverters. Microinverters improve efficiency and allow for panel-level optimization, but they are more expensive than traditional string inverters.
  4. Installation Complexity – If your roof has multiple angles, shading issues, or structural challenges, installation costs may increase. Complex installations require additional labor, mounting equipment, and electrical adjustments.
  5. Battery Storage –  Adding a solar panel battery increases the overall system cost, but it allows homeowners to store excess energy and reduce reliance on the grid.

Why 6.6kW is the Most Popular System Size in Perth

The 6.6kW solar system has become the standard choice for Perth homeowners due to its cost-effectiveness and efficiency. It strikes the perfect balance between affordability and power output.

  • Produces around 26kWh per day, which is enough to cover the electricity needs of an average home.
  • Affordable pricing – With government rebates, homeowners can install a 6.6kW system for around $5,000 to $8,500.
  • Compatible with a 5kVA inverter, which is the limit for single-phase homes in WA.
  • Short payback period – Homeowners can recoup their investment within 3 to 4 years, thanks to reduced electricity bills.

Government Rebates & Perth Solar Incentives

Perth homeowners can take advantage of solar rebates and incentives to lower the cost of solar panels.

Federal Government Incentives

The Small-scale Renewable Energy Scheme (SRES) provides Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs) to reduce upfront solar panel costs. The number of STCs you receive depends on your system size and location.

  • A 6.6kW solar system typically receives between $2,500 and $3,000 worth of STCs, significantly lowering the installation cost.
  • The STC value decreases annually, so installing solar sooner ensures greater financial benefits.

Western Australia Solar Rebates

The Renewable Energy Buyback Scheme (REBS) enables homeowners to sell excess solar power back to the grid. The feed-in tariff rate varies based on your energy retailer and time of day.

  • Homeowners receive between 3 and 10 cents per kilowatt-hour for surplus electricity exported to the grid.
  • Choosing a battery storage system allows you to maximize self-consumption instead of selling power at a lower rate.

Solar Energy Costs vs. Long-Term Savings

Many homeowners ask whether solar panels in Perth are a worthwhile investment. The answer is a resounding yes due to the significant long-term savings they provide. While there is an initial cost, the savings on electricity bills can amount to thousands of dollars per year.

Estimated Annual Savings on Electricity Bills with Solar Panels in Perth

  • A 5kW system reduces energy costs by $1,200 to $1,800 per year, covering most or all of a medium household's electricity needs.

  • A 10kW system provides savings of $2,500 or more per year, making it ideal for high-energy users or large households.

How Long Does It Take to Break Even on Solar in Perth?

The payback period for solar panels in Perth depends on system size, energy consumption, and how much solar power is self-consumed.

  • A 3kW system typically pays for itself in 4 to 6 years.
  • A 5kW system has a payback period of 3 to 5 years.
  • A 6.6kW system usually breaks even within 3 to 4 years.
  • A 10kW system can recoup its costs in 3 to 4 years, especially for large households that consume a significant amount of electricity.
