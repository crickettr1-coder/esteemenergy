What is a 13kW Solar System?

A 13kW solar system is considered a large residential or small commercial setup, ideal for homes with high daily electricity use or businesses with consistent daytime consumption. The system size is powerful enough to cover substantial energy loads, including electric vehicle charging, heating/cooling systems, and general household use. Here’s what a typical 13kW system includes:
  • Power output: 13,000 watts (13kW)
  • Daily generation: 50–60 kWh depending on location and panel orientation
  • Yearly output: Around 18,000 – 21,000 kWh
  • Panels needed: Approximately 30–36, depending on individual panel wattage
  • Roof space required: About 65 to 75 square metres
The 13 kilowatt solar setup price can vary depending on component quality, local installation costs, and available rebates. It’s an ideal solution for homes with daily electricity usage over 40kWh and enough roof space to install the system safely and effectively.

Average Cost of a 13kW Solar System in Australia (2025)

The average cost of 13kW solar system in Australia in 2025 ranges between $13,000 and $18,000. This price range includes full installation by a CEC-accredited installer, high-efficiency solar panels, and a quality inverter. The final 13kW system price depends on several elements, including panel brand, inverter type, location, and whether or not you include a battery. Here's how pricing usually breaks down:
  • Standard system: $13,000 – $15,000 with mid-range panels and string inverter
  • Premium system: $15,500 – $18,000 with high-end panels, hybrid inverter, and smart monitoring
  • Battery add-on: An additional $8,000 – $15,000 if added at the time of installation
If you’re comparing different quotes, remember that the large solar system cost should not just reflect price, but also long-term value, warranties, and installation quality. Always request a detailed quote from a trusted provider like Esteem Energy, and make sure CEC accreditation is guaranteed.

Factors That Affect the Cost of 13kW Solar System

The cost of 13kW solar system is influenced by multiple factors, not just hardware. Here are the main components that shape your final price:
  • Location: Regions with higher sun exposure yield more energy, improving ROI and allowing for a smaller system in some cases. Installation costs may also differ by state.
  • Roof condition and design: Tiled, multi-level, or shaded roofs may increase labor time and mounting complexity.
  • Panel and inverter brands: Premium brands like LG, SunPower, or Fronius tend to cost more but offer longer warranties and better efficiency.
  • Government rebates: The federal STC scheme can reduce your upfront price by several thousand dollars depending on your zone.
  • Battery inclusion: Adding battery storage will increase the 13 kilowatt solar setup price, but also increases self-consumption and blackout protection.
  • Installer expertise: Using a CEC-accredited installer ensures quality and eligibility for rebates.
All these factors can shift the 13kW system price, so it's essential to get a custom quote based on your property and energy usage.

Expected Savings & Payback Period

When installed correctly, a 13kW solar system can significantly reduce your electricity bills and provide an excellent return on investment. Here’s what you can expect:
  • Daily savings: $8 to $13, depending on your energy rates and usage habits
  • Annual savings: Around $3,000 to $4,500 per year
  • Payback period: Typically between 4 to 6 years
  • Lifetime savings: Over $50,000 across 25+ years, even more if energy prices rise
The cost of 13kW solar system may seem high initially, but when you consider these ongoing savings, the value becomes clear. Your ROI improves even further if you self-consume more of the energy rather than exporting it to the grid. A home using 50kWh per day could offset up to 90% of its electricity bills, making the 13kW system price an investment that pays for itself within just a few years.

Is a 13kW Solar System Right for You?

A 13kW solar system is ideal if:
  • Your household uses over 40–50kWh of electricity daily
  • You have a large family, run a business from home, or use high-power appliances
  • You own or plan to own an electric vehicle
  • Your home features ducted air conditioning, a pool, or a spa
  • You have ample roof space and good solar exposure
Although the large solar system cost is higher than smaller systems, it can be more cost-effective for high-usage homes over the long term. It’s also future-proof, supporting battery upgrades and extra energy needs as your household grows.
