Solar Panel Installation: What Homeowners Should Expect

A step-by-step walkthrough of the residential installation timeline, safety standards, and grid-connection approvals required for Australian homes.

Switching your home to solar is an exciting milestone, but many homeowners feel uncertain about what happens on the actual day of installation. Knowing what to expect during the installation process ensures a smooth timeline and helps you prepare your property. Here is our complete step-by-step walkthrough of the residential installation journey.

1. Pre-Installation Site Assessment

Before any panels arrive on your roof, a thorough site engineering assessment is conducted. This process evaluates your roof's structural integrity, structural shading, and current electrical infrastructure. Our design team will compile the final layout and submit connection paperwork to your local electricity distributor to obtain pre-approval.

2. The Installation Day Timeline

On the scheduled installation day, our CEC-accredited installation team will arrive with the hardware. For a standard 6.6 kW residential installation, the process typically takes one business day (usually between 5 to 8 hours) and proceeds as follows:

  • Site Setup & Safety: The team sets up scaffolding or safety harness lines to comply with strict Australian occupational health and safety rules.
  • Mounting Rail Installation: Racking brackets are secured to your roof rafters, and aluminum rails are aligned.
  • Panel Fitting: Solar panels are lifted onto the roof, clamped to the mounting rails, and wired in series.
  • Inverter Installation: The solar inverter (usually 5kW for a 6.6kW system) is mounted on a clean garage or external wall near your switchboard.
  • Electrical Wiring: The system is wired to your home switchboard, and safety switches are integrated.
"Standard single-phase installations are usually completed in 1 day, while complex multi-story properties or split-roof orientations may require a second day for precision alignment."

3. Grid Inspection and Connection Paperwork

Once physically installed, the system cannot be turned on immediately. A licensed electrical inspector must inspect the wiring and issue a Certificate of Electrical Safety (CES). We then submit the compliance paperwork to your electricity retailer to re-program your electricity meter into a "Smart Meter" or "Bi-directional Meter". This meter tracks both the power you draw and the excess solar energy you export back to the grid for credits.

4. Handover & System Operation

Once connection approvals are finalized, the team will walk you through the system switch-on procedure and show you how to download and use the companion smart monitoring app. This application allows you to track real-time solar generation, home usage, and battery storage levels directly from your smartphone.
