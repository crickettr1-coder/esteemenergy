Maximizing Your Solar Savings with the Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff

If you're considering making the switch to solar energy, one of the most important aspects to understand is the Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff. Feed-in tariffs (FITs) provide a way for homeowners to earn credits for the surplus energy their solar panels generate and send back to the grid. This payment or credit can significantly reduce your electricity bills and increase savings.

With the Origin Energy solar feed-in rates being competitive and flexible, it's important to understand how they work, how much you could earn from the Origin solar feed-in tariff, and how it compares to other options available in Australia. In this article, we’ll explore how to make the most of Origin solar credits, solar rebates, and the best feed-in tariffs in Australia to maximize your energy savings.

What is a Solar Feed-in Tariff?

A solar feed-in tariff (FIT) is a payment made to solar system owners for the excess solar energy they produce and send back into the national grid. When your solar system generates more electricity than you need, the surplus energy is exported to the grid, and you receive a feed-in tariff for that electricity.

Here’s a quick overview of how the Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff works:

  • Exporting Excess Energy: When your solar panels generate more power than you consume, the excess power is sent back to the grid.
  • Earning Credits: The energy provider (in this case, Origin) compensates you based on the Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff rate for the electricity exported.
  • Reducing Energy Bills: These credits directly offset the cost of your electricity, which can drastically reduce your overall energy bills.

This simple concept helps homeowners maximize solar savings by earning money or credits from the solar energy they are already producing.

Understanding Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff Plans

Origin Energy offers several solar plans with different feed-in tariffs to accommodate various customer needs. Here are the most popular Origin solar plans:

  • Origin Solar Boost: This plan offers higher feed-in tariffs for customers who export a significant amount of solar energy back to the grid. This plan is ideal if you have a larger system, like a 6.6 kW solar system, or produce more energy than you consume. 
  • Origin Solar Boost Plus: Customers who have an Origin-installed system can enjoy additional solar incentives with this plan. Not only do you benefit from higher feed-in rates, but you also receive extra rewards for having a professionally installed system.
  • Origin Solar Lite: A simpler plan that provides a stable feed-in rate, although the rate is lower compared to the Solar Boost plan. It's a good option for those with smaller systems like a 5 kW solar system or who don’t export a lot of energy.

How much does Origin pay for solar feed-in? The rates vary depending on your plan and location, but Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff rates are generally competitive, ensuring that you earn a fair price for your exported solar energy.

Maximizing Your Solar Savings with Origin Energy

To get the most out of your Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff, there are several ways to maximize your solar credits:

  • Track Your Energy Export: By monitoring your solar energy export, you can adjust your consumption habits to ensure you’re exporting as much as possible. The more energy you export, the more Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff credits you earn.
  • Choose the Right Plan: Depending on your solar energy production, choosing the right plan is crucial. If you have a larger system, such as a 13 kW solar system, and export more energy, the Origin Solar Boost plan may be the best option.
  • Battery Storage: Adding battery storage to your solar system allows you to store excess energy for later use, reducing reliance on the grid and ensuring that you maximize your solar savings. Additionally, Origin solar rebates can help reduce the cost of installing a battery system.

By taking these steps, you can ensure that you're getting the most out of the Origin Solar Feed-in Tariff, while boosting your overall savings from your solar system.
