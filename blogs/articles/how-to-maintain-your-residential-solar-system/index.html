How to Maintain Your Residential Solar System

Simple guidelines for maintaining panel efficiency, monitoring system health, and scheduling professional inspections.

One of the primary benefits of residential solar power is that it requires very little day-to-day upkeep. Solar panels are solid-state technology with no moving parts, meaning they are built to last for decades with minimal intervention. However, performing basic maintenance checks can prevent output degradation and ensure your system continues to run at maximum efficiency. Here is what you need to know.

1. Keep Your Panels Clean

Over time, dirt, dust, pollen, bird droppings, and leaves can accumulate on the surface of your solar panels, blocking sunlight and reducing their conversion efficiency. While rainfall naturally washes away most loose debris, a periodic manual clean is recommended:

  • Safety First: Never climb onto a steep or wet roof yourself. If your panels are hard to reach, hire a professional cleaning service.
  • Soft Cleaning Tools: Use a soft-bristled brush or squeegee with a hose. Avoid abrasive sponges, high-pressure washers, or harsh chemical detergents, which can scratch or damage the anti-reflective glass coating.
  • Optimal Timing: Clean panels early in the morning or late in the afternoon when they are cool. Spraying cold water onto hot glass in the middle of a summer day can cause thermal shock and crack the panels.

2. Monitor Your Inverter and Energy Output

Your inverter is the brain of your solar system. Regularly checking its status helps you identify issues before they impact your utility bills:

  • Status Indicator Lights: A steady green light typically indicates the system is generating power normally. Red or amber lights indicate a fault code, which should be inspected by a technician.
  • App Monitoring: Check your system monitoring app weekly. If you notice a sudden drop in generation that cannot be explained by rainy or overcast weather, it may indicate a panel string outage or inverter issue.
"Clean panels and active monitoring are the most effective ways to prevent system degradation and secure your expected electricity savings year after year."

3. Schedule Professional Electrical Inspections

While basic cleaning is simple, your solar system runs on high-voltage DC and AC electricity. We recommend scheduling a professional system service every 2 to 5 years. A qualified CEC-accredited technician will check:

  1. Racking mounts and panel integrity for any cracks or degradation.
  2. Wiring terminals and conduit for weathering or animal damage.
  3. DC isolator switches to ensure they remain water-tight and functional.
  4. Switchboard electrical connections and safety switch operation.
