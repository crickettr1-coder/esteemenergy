What is a 6.6kW Solar System with a 5kW Inverter?

A 6.6kW solar system consists of a set of solar panels that together generate 6.6 kilowatts of electricity. The solar panels convert sunlight into direct current (DC) electricity. The 5kW inverter then converts this DC power into alternating current (AC), the form of electricity your home uses.

The inverter sizing is a key aspect of solar system efficiency. In Australia, a 5kW inverter paired with a 6.6kW solar system is a common configuration due to the Clean Energy Council’s allowance for oversizing—the process of using an inverter that is slightly smaller than the total panel output. This setup optimizes the system’s performance, particularly in varying weather conditions, and ensures energy is generated efficiently.

Understanding Inverter Sizing: Why Not Match 6.6kW to 6.6kW?

It may seem logical to pair a 6.6kW solar system with a 6.6kW inverter, but this is not always the most efficient option. Here's why:

  • Performance Optimization: A 5kW inverter ensures that excess energy produced during peak sunlight hours does not go to waste. By slightly undersizing the inverter, you avoid clipping, which occurs when the inverter can’t handle excess power.

  • Improved Return on Investment (ROI): Oversizing with a 5kW inverter can actually improve the system’s ROI by maximizing performance when sunlight levels vary. This setup reduces wasted energy and increases the amount of electricity that can be sent back to the grid.

  • Better Performance in Real-World Conditions: Weather in Australia is not always sunny, and partial shading can reduce solar panel output. The 6.6kW solar system with 5kW inverter price is designed to handle such fluctuations, delivering better long-term savings.

Hybrid Solar System Option: Should You Add Battery Storage?

If you want to further maximize the potential of your 6.6kW solar system with a 5kW inverter, consider adding a hybrid solar system with a solar panel battery. This allows you to store excess solar power generated during the day for use at night or during cloudy periods.

While battery storage adds to the residential solar cost, it can be highly beneficial if you have high energy consumption during off-peak hours or want to protect your home from blackouts. There are also several state and federal incentives available for adding batteries, which can help reduce the cost.
