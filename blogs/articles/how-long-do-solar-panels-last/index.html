How Long Do Solar Panels Last?

If you’re wondering, how long do solar panels last, the answer is simple: solar panels typically last between 25 and 30 years. This long-lasting investment allows homeowners to enjoy clean, renewable energy while significantly reducing electricity costs. However, the actual solar panel lifespan depends on factors such as the quality of the panels, installation, and environmental conditions.

In this guide, we will answer the question how long do solar panels last, explore key factors that influence their durability, and provide tips on how to maintain your panels for optimal performance. By understanding how long solar panels last, you’ll be better equipped to maximize your energy savings and ensure the longevity of your residential solar panel system.

What is the Average Lifespan of Solar Panels?

So, how long do solar panels last on average? The expected lifespan of solar technology is impressive, with most panels retaining significant efficiency even after decades of use. Here’s what to know:

  • Degradation Rates Matter: Solar panels typically degrade at a rate of 0.3% to 0.8% annually. Over 25 years, most panels operate at 80% to 90% of their original capacity. High-quality panels, like those from SunPower or Panasonic, tend to degrade more slowly.
  • Longevity Stats: With proper care, many solar systems exceed 30 years, continuing to generate electricity, albeit at reduced efficiency.

While panels may not stop working outright after their expected lifespan, their reduced output may impact your energy goals. Understanding performance degradation of solar panels helps you plan maintenance and upgrades effectively.

Factors That Affect Solar Panel Longevity

Several key factors determine how long solar panels last and how efficiently they perform over time. By understanding these, you can better maintain your system and ensure it reaches its full lifespan.

  1. Degradation Rates: A critical factor in how long solar panels last is their degradation rate. Panels from premium manufacturers such as Panasonic or SunPower tend to degrade more slowly, at rates as low as 0.3% per year. On the other hand, lower-quality panels may degrade faster, at rates of 0.8% or higher, reducing their efficiency sooner.
  2. Environmental Conditions: Where you live also plays a major role in how long solar panels last. In regions like Australia, where UV exposure is high, the panels may degrade faster due to the intense sunlight. Other factors like wind, humidity, and temperature fluctuations also contribute to a panel's lifespan. Proper installation and regular maintenance can help mitigate these environmental effects and prolong solar panel longevity.
  3. Installation Practices: Another factor that impacts how long solar panels last is the quality of the installation. Poor installation can result in microcracks or inefficient placement, which reduces the effectiveness of the panels over time. Properly installed panels, with adequate airflow and optimal positioning, help maintain solar panel durability for years to come.
  4. Maintenance for Long-Life Solar Panels: While solar panels require little maintenance, regular cleaning and inspections are necessary to ensure their longevity. Dust, debris, and bird droppings can reduce the efficiency of your system, so keeping your panels clean is essential. Inspections for physical damage, such as cracks or loose connections, also help extend the solar panel lifespan.

Understanding Warranties on Solar Systems 

When assessing how long do solar panels last, it’s important to understand the warranties that come with them. Warranties give you an idea of the expected solar panel life expectancy.

  • Performance Warranty: Most solar panels for home come with a 25-year performance warranty, which ensures that the panels will still generate at least 80% of their original capacity after 25 years. This is a good indicator of how long solar panels last and how much efficiency you can expect over time.
  • Product Warranty: The product warranty typically covers defects in materials and workmanship for 10 to 25 years. Panels with longer product warranties tend to be higher quality, offering greater solar panel durability.

When to Replace Solar Panels

As your solar panels age, you may start to notice signs that indicate they need to be replaced. Knowing these signs can help you determine how long your solar panels last and when it's time to consider new ones.

  1. Decreased Energy Output: If your energy output drops significantly despite clean and undamaged panels, it could be due to the aging system. As panels degrade, their ability to generate power diminishes.
  2. Physical Damage: Cracks, warping, or discolouration are signs of physical damage that can impact how long solar panels last. Damaged panels are less efficient and may need to be replaced sooner than expected.
  3. Higher Energy Bills: If your energy bills are rising after years of savings, it could indicate that your solar panel life expectancy has been reached and that it's time for an upgrade.

Tips to Maximize Solar Panel Lifespan

To ensure your solar panels last as long as possible, it’s important to follow certain maintenance practices. Here are some tips to extend how long solar panels last:

  1. Regular Cleaning: Dust, leaves, and debris can block sunlight and reduce the efficiency of your panels.  Cleaning your panels regularly helps them perform at their best and extend their lifespan. Explore the best way to clean solar panels for more understanding. 
  2. Monitor Energy Output: Use a solar monitoring app or system to keep track of how much energy your panels are producing. Any sudden drops in performance could signal an issue that needs addressing.
  3. Inspect for Damage: Regularly inspecting your panels for physical damage, such as cracks or corrosion, can prevent further deterioration and help maintain solar panel durability.
  4. Choose Quality Panels and Installers: Investing in high-quality panels and solar panel installation ensures your system is built to last, maximizing the solar panel life expectancy and efficiency over time.

Tracking Longevity Stats and Performance Degradation

Monitoring performance degradation of solar panels over time helps homeowners understand how their system is aging. On average, panels lose 0.3% to 0.8% of efficiency each year. After 25 years, most panels still operate between 80% and 90% of their original capacity. Reviewing longevity stats from manufacturers or independent studies can guide your choice of panels to ensure long-lasting performance.

Expected Lifespan of Solar Tech: A Reliable Investment

The expected lifespan of solar tech makes it a sound investment for both residential and commercial applications. With proper care, solar systems often exceed their estimated operational life, continuing to provide renewable energy at a reduced capacity. By choosing high-quality products and maintaining your system diligently, you can enjoy clean energy for decades.

Conclusion

Solar panels are an excellent long-term investment, with most systems lasting between 25 and 30 years. Understanding how long solar panels last depends on several factors, including degradation rates, environmental conditions, and regular maintenance. With proper care and the right materials, you can ensure your system continues to provide clean, renewable energy for decades, maximizing your savings and environmental impact.

Frequently Asked Questions​

Q1. How long do solar panels last?

Solar panels typically last between 25 and 30 years, although they can continue producing energy beyond this period.

Q2. Do solar panels stop working after 30 years?

No, solar panels continue to work after 30 years, but their efficiency will decrease. The average degradation rate means they will produce less electricity as they age.

Q3. What factors affect how long solar panels last?

Factors such as degradation rates, environmental conditions, and proper installation all influence how long solar panels last.

Q4. Can you extend the lifespan of solar panels?

Yes, with proper maintenance, regular cleaning, and choosing high-quality materials, you can extend the solar panel lifespan.

Q5. How often should I clean my solar panels?

You should clean your panels at least once or twice a year to remove dust and debris, ensuring they operate efficiently and prolonging how long solar panels last.

Q6. What happens when solar panels degrade?

When solar panels degrade, they lose efficiency in converting sunlight to electricity. This is a gradual process, and most panels still function at 80% to 90% efficiency after 25 years.

Q7. Do solar panels come with warranties?

Yes, most solar panels come with a 25-year performance warranty and a product warranty that covers defects for 10 to 25 years.

Q8. When should I replace my solar panels?

Solar panels should be replaced if they no longer meet your energy needs, show physical damage, or have significantly reduced efficiency after reaching the end of their solar panel life expectancy.
