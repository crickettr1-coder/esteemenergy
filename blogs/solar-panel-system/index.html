Early Use of Solar Energy for Steam Production:

The application of solar energy in the 19th century was limited to usage for producing steam. It was mainly designed to concentrate more light on a boiler for making steam, which was helpful in various uses in industries like driving and heating machinery.

Edmond Becquerel’s Photovoltaic Effect:

The history of photovoltaics dates back to 1839 when a French physicist, Edmond Becquerel, came up with the photovoltaic effect. This phenomenon includes the production of an electric current when there are materials that have been exposed to light. Still, his discovery was momentous, but it will take several decades before the advent of solar cellular technology that stemmed from his research.

Charles Fritts and the First Solar Cell:

It was invented by an American named Charles Fritts in 1883, albeit an improvised solar cell with meager efficiency. Nevertheless, the cell was not so efficient in comparison with the previous one and was created using a thin layer of selenium covered with gold. This was not accepted as a technology of solar cells because of the drawbacks that came with it.

Russel Ohl and Silicon Solar Cells:

Russel Ohl, while working at Bell Laboratories in the 1940s, played the final initial critical role in inventing the silicon solar cell process, which was patented in 1941. They help give way to becoming more efficient than previous attempts concerning the modern evolution of solar panels.

Solar Panels:

Whether it is referred to as a PV Panel or a Photovoltaic Panel, they both represent the same product. It is a device that is utilized to change the sunlight directly into electricity generated on its surface by particles, which are mainly called photons and is used to power electric loads. It is suitable for different and broad uses, such as in the production of power systems for cabins and other types of telecommunication equipment and remote sensing and, above all, in the generation of power for use in residential/commercial sources.

How do Solar Panels work?:

Solar Panel Composition:

These are mini electric cells that form the solar panel system and are made of silicon and also layers of phosphorous and boron. These materials cooperate in order to help undergo the process of conversion of sunlight into electricity, which is the basis of the PV effect.

Photovoltaic Effect:

The photovoltaic effect is the basic mechanism by which solar panels work and is a crucial process in the conversion of light to electrical energy. Semiconductor materials are used in the construction of solar panels where photons from sunlight cause the electrons to loosen or move, thus providing an electricity flow. Grid-Connected Systems:

In a grid-connected solar panel system, the net metering programs can inject the surplus generated electricity back into the main electric grid in the evening. This enables homeowners to get paid or credit for the excess energy that is generated so as to counterbalance their energy consumption, particularly during the night or on days when there is low sunlight.

Off-Grid Systems:

Due to the off-grid solar panel system, extra components will be installed, such as the battery pack in which excess energy produced is stored, the charge controller that regulates the battery charging process, and the inverter which converts the DC current stored in batteries to the AC current needed for uses in homes or elsewhere.
