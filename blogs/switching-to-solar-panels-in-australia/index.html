Key Components of Solar Panels:

1. Photovoltaic Cells (PV Cells):

Embedded in the principle of the solar panel are a number of photovoltaic cells normally formed from semiconductor material such as silicon. These cells convert sunlight directly to direct current (DC) electricity.

2. Semiconductors and Electron Movement:

How Solar Panels Work:

Sunlight Absorption: While these systems are called solar panels, the panels are located in a manner that they capture sunlight. Photons, which are particles of light, cause electrons within the PV components to become activated when they come into contact with the photovoltaic cell panels. Generation of Electrons: The energy makes electrons from some of the atoms within the semiconductor material free to generate an electric current output.

1. Direct Current (DC) Electricity:*

The generated electric current is direct current (DC), where electrons flow in a single direction. However, most household and industrial appliances use alternating current (AC).

2. Inverter Conversion:

Inverter is an electrical device that converts direct current (DC) electricity to an alternating current, AC form, so that the electricity is suitable for use within homes and industries.

3. Grid Connection or Storage:

The converted AC electricity which is produced can be used to drive electrical equipment in real time. Any surplus power that is produced can be either sold back to the electricity company or deposited in batteries for use later.

4. Net Metering (Optional):

In the grid-connected system, the net metering policy ensures that the users can be rewarded for the excess electricity produced. In particular, the conventional electricity generation method can be bridged with the aid of the electric grid during low power demand.
