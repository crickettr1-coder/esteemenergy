Common Myths To Avoid and The Reason Behind Solar Systems

2023-12-05 11:33:15•By EsteemEnergy_AU
According to Climate of the Nation 2022, approximately 76% of Australians rank in the top 1 tier solars in the top three preferred energy sources. Australia is the world's leader in solar uptake per capita, with approximately 1 kW of solar installed per citizen. Apart from this, many myths might discourage owners from choosing solar systems on their property, but here in this article, we will overview the truth behind the myths and Knows About What is the Reason Behind Solar Systems.

Myth 1. Solar Systems is Too Expensive:

Truth:

Some federal and state incentives help reduce the total cost of renewable energy. STCs (Small Scale Technology certificate). It is available in every state of Australia, which helps save hundreds and thousands of dollars on your new purchase. STCs can be combined also with state incentives to reduce further Solar Systems Costs.

Myth 2. Solar Systems doesn't work in cloudy or rainy seasons:

Truth:

Solar Power Systems can work even when there is no electricity, or the sun is not shining. It totally depends on the amount of cloud covered; some of the sun’s energy should still get through to operate solar power or solar hot water systems. Even if there are many cloudy days weather, there are ways to get most of the system, which are:
  • Solar water heaters have an electric or gas backup booster to ensure you can still enjoy hot water when needed.
  • The efficiency of solar power systems may be reduced, but you will still have power from the grid to fall back on or excess solar energy if you have a battery installed.

Myth 3. Solars Systems aren't efficient:

Truth:

The sun produces enough energy in an hour to provide power to every person on Earth for a whole year. However, to maximize your solar investment, you must ensure the panels you select are high quality, reliable, and durable. Regarding solar quality, Esteem Energy will ensure their selection of panels meets strict quality standards and is tested for optimum performance and efficiency. Hence, your system works to deliver a reliable and efficient power supply.

Myth 4. Don't use much power in the day, so there is no need for Solars:

Truth:

The rise in working households often means less power is consumed during the day. However, this doesn't mean you can't use solar energy. With the help of the Esteem Energy app, you can see when your solar power system generates the most power and operates high-consuming appliances such as water heaters and pool pumps to help you save more on your energy bills. Adding a solar panel battery storage system to new or existing solar power systems allows you to store your unused solar energy to use later and reduce your reliance on the grid. This includes:
  • At night
  • On low sunlight days
  • When utility rates are more expensive
  • During blackouts

Myth 5. Can you have renewable energy if your roof is small?

Truth:

A heat pump water heater is a great option if your roof is unsuitable for solar. Instead of the sun, it uses the surrounding air to heat water, which removes the need for solar collectors. Also, it is an easy replacement for an electric water heater, as heat pumps often use existing plumbing and electrical connections. If there are freezing conditions, a well-qualified heat pump should have an electric booster function that will be triggered to ensure a continuous hot water supply.

Myth 6: Solar Systems Aren't Effective in High Latitudes

Truth:

Solar panels can still generate significant power in higher latitudes. At the same time, they may not receive the same amount of sunlight as equatorial regions; modern solar systems are designed to capture and convert available sunlight efficiently. In high-latitude areas, proper system design and placement can maximize energy production.

Myth 7: Solar Panels Harm the Environment

Truth:

Solar panels are environmentally friendly and contribute to reducing carbon footprints. The production of solar panels has become more sustainable, and their energy payback period—the time it takes for a panel to generate as much energy as was used in its production—has decreased significantly. Solar energy helps combat climate change and reduces dependence on fossil fuels.

Myth 8: Solar Panels Have a Short Lifespan

Truth:

Solar panels are built to last. Most solar panels have warranties that guarantee performance for 25 years or more. While their efficiency may decrease slightly over time, they continue to generate power well beyond their warranty period. Regular maintenance and advancements in technology contribute to the longevity of solar panels.

Myth 9: Solar Systems Are Only for Wealthy Homeowners

Truth:

The perception that solar systems are only for the wealthy is outdated. With various financing options, government incentives, and falling installation costs, solar energy is accessible to a broader range of homeowners. Many solar providers offer financing plans that make it feasible for homeowners with different budget considerations.

Myth 10: Solar Systems Don't Add Value to Homes

Truth:

Homes with solar panel installations often have increased market value. Solar systems are viewed as desirable features, especially as energy efficiency becomes a priority for homebuyers. Studies have shown that homes with solar panels sell faster and at higher prices than comparable non-solar homes.

Conclusion:

debunking these common myths reveals that solar energy is a viable, cost-effective, and environmentally friendly solution for powering homes and businesses. As technology advances and awareness grows, solar systems play a crucial role in transitioning to a sustainable energy future. In conclusion, solar energy is a viable and cost-effective option for Australian homeowners, and many common myths surrounding it can be debunked with the correct information. With technological advancements and various incentives available, solar systems significantly reduce energy bills and environmental impact.
