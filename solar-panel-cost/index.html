How Much Do Solar Panels Cost? A Complete Guide to Solar System Installation Costs in Australia

If you're searching for a clear breakdown of solar panel cost in Australia, you're in the right place. Most homeowners can expect to pay between $4,000 and $15,000, depending on the size and quality of the system. This article gives you a full understanding of how much you’ll actually spend, what impacts that cost, and how to maximise your investment. With electricity prices continuing to rise, interest in solar energy is soaring across Australia. But before you commit, it’s important to understand exactly what goes into your solar system installation cost, from equipment and brand quality to government rebates and local factors. Whether you're installing solar for the first time or upgrading an existing setup, this guide simplifies the numbers and helps you make an informed decision about your total solar panel cost.

What Is the Average Solar Panel Cost in Australia?

When Australians talk about solar panel cost, they usually mean the entire system, including the panels, inverter, mounting hardware, and professional installation — not just the panels themselves. In 2025, the average cost of a complete solar power system ranges significantly based on size and product quality. Here's what you can typically expect to pay:
  • 3kW solar system: Between $4,000 and $5,000
  • 6.6kW solar system: Between $5,500 and $8,000
  • 10kW solar system: Between $9,000 and $13,000
  • 13.3kW solar system: Between $11,500 and $15,000
The solar cost per kW generally decreases with larger systems. For example, a 6.6kW system might cost around $1,050 per kW, while a 3kW system might cost over $1,300 per kW. These numbers include government rebates and reflect a standard rooftop installation in metro areas. It’s important to get quotes tailored to your property, but knowing the national averages gives you a strong foundation when evaluating your personal solar panel cost.

Key Factors That Influence Solar System Installation Cost

1. System Size (kW)

One of the most significant variables in your solar panel cost is the size of the system. The more kilowatts (kW) you install, the higher the upfront cost — but your solar cost per kW usually decreases. That means a larger system can offer better value per watt. For instance:
  • A 6.6kW system suits most medium-sized households.
  • A 10kW system works well for families with high power usage or electric vehicles.
  • A 13.3kW system is ideal for those looking to maximise savings, add batteries, or support business usage.
Esteem Energy offers a range of system sizes tailored to your energy needs and budget.

2. Price of Solar Panels

The price of solar panels can vary significantly based on brand, quality, and technology. Budget brands might offer a lower initial investment, but premium panels typically offer better efficiency, warranties, and long-term performance. Here’s a general comparison:
  • Budget brands (e.g., some models from Jinko or Canadian Solar): Cheaper, lower efficiency, 10–15 year warranties.
  • Mid-range brands (e.g., Trina, Risen): Balanced price and performance, up to 25-year warranties.
  • Premium brands (e.g., SunPower, REC): Higher efficiency, long warranties, best long-term value.
Choosing a reputable brand will influence your total solar system installation cost, but also affects your return on investment over time.

3. Type of Panels and Inverters

The type of technology you choose for your system can change your overall solar panel cost significantly. Panel types:
  • Monocrystalline panels: High efficiency, sleek black look, higher cost.
  • Polycrystalline panels: Cheaper, slightly less efficient, blue tone.
  • Thin-film panels: Rare in residential setups, more flexible, but lower output.
Inverter types:
  • String inverters: Most common and cost-effective.
  • Microinverters: Installed on each panel, great for shade-prone roofs, slightly more expensive.
  • Hybrid inverters: Ready for battery integration, ideal for future-proofing.
Premium inverters like Fronius or SolarEdge can improve performance and reliability, especially when paired with high-end solar panels.

4. Installation Complexity

Every home is unique, and certain site conditions can increase your solar system installation cost. These factors may increase labour or equipment costs:
  • Steep or double-storey roofs
  • Shaded rooftops that require special panel layout
  • Switchboard upgrades or rewiring
  • Tile or fragile roof materials
  • Rural or hard-to-access properties
An experienced installer like Esteem Energy will assess your roof design, energy usage, and structural conditions before quoting your final solar panel cost.

5. Location and Local Rebates

Your geographic location plays a huge role in both the price of solar panels and the final cost of installation. Urban areas like Sydney, Melbourne, or Brisbane tend to have competitive installer pricing due to high demand and installer availability. Regional and rural areas might see slightly higher prices due to added transport and limited local competition. Your location also affects the number of Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs) your system qualifies for — which leads us into the next section.

How the Solar Rebate Works

Australia’s solar rebate, also known as the STC program, directly reduces your upfront solar panel cost. Here’s how it works:
  • STCs are awarded based on your location and system size.
  • The more efficient your system and sunnier your region, the more STCs you’ll receive.
  • Each STC has a market value — usually between $35 and $39 — and this value is deducted from your quote.
For example, a 6.6kW system in Sydney might generate around 90 STCs. At $36 per certificate, that’s a $3,240 discount applied immediately to reduce your solar system installation cost. Esteem Energy includes this rebate in every quote, so you don’t need to claim it yourself.

Premium vs Budget: Is Paying More Worth It?

It can be tempting to go with the cheapest option, but when it comes to solar panel cost, lower prices often mean lower efficiency, shorter warranties, and reduced lifespan. Here’s why premium systems offer better value:
  • Better efficiency: More power output from the same roof space
  • Longer warranties: Up to 25 years on panels and 10–12 years on inverters
  • Higher quality: Less likelihood of breakdowns or performance loss
  • Greater long-term savings: More reliable output = bigger reductions in energy bills
A budget system may cost less initially but might need costly repairs or upgrades sooner. Investing in quality upfront can reduce your total lifetime solar panel cost and increase your return on investment.

How to Calculate Your Expected Savings

Understanding how much money you'll save with solar is just as important as knowing the solar panel cost. Here’s how to estimate your savings:
  1. Determine your daily energy usage (in kWh) — found on your electricity bill.
  2. Match a system size that suits your needs (e.g., 6.6kW generates around 24–27kWh/day).
  3. Calculate how much energy you'll offset — most homes save 50–80% on power bills.
  4. Account for feed-in tariffs, which pay you for excess power sent to the grid.
Typical savings range from $800 to $2,500 per year, depending on usage and system size. The average payback period for a solar system is 3 to 5 years — after that, you're generating free electricity.

  • Warranties: At least 10 years on workmanship and 25 years on panels
  • Transparency: Clear quotes, honest recommendations, no pushy sales tactics
  • Support: Ongoing service, maintenance, and warranty assistance

The Smart Way to Reduce Your Solar Costs

Understanding your full solar panel cost helps you make a confident and informed decision. From system size and rebates to the price of solar panels and quality of installation, many variables can impact your total investment. To maximise your savings:
